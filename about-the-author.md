# About the Author

**Nick Meyering** is a civic technologist, Disabled transit advocate, and public policy practitioner based in Philadelphia, PA.

-----

## The Personal Stakes in This Methodology

I was born Disabled. My wife acquired disabilities and is now chronically ill. We live in Philadelphia and depend on SEPTA — a transit system that is underfunded, aging, and increasingly climate-stressed — to get to medical appointments, to work, to community. That is not background information. That is why every methodological decision in this index is made with Disabled communities as a named, explicit priority rather than a footnote.

When I designed the Social Vulnerability Index weight (the highest in the CBI at 25%), I was thinking about what it means to not be able to evacuate during a flood because the accessible transit routes are flooded. When I wrote the Disability Disparity Audit in Notebook 03, I was thinking about the emergency management plans that list “people with disabilities” in a bullet point but were written without a single Disabled person in the room.

The CBI is a data tool. But data tools are made by people, and people have stakes. Mine are on the table.

## A Note on Transparency

I am not a climate scientist. I am not a professional data scientist. I am a public policy practitioner with civic tech skills, lived experience with climate-intersecting vulnerabilities, and a commitment to building tools that communities can actually use.

Every methodology decision in this index is documented and open to critique. I have made judgment calls — on weights, on variable selection, on how to handle missing data — and I have tried to explain every one of them. If you disagree with a decision, open an issue. That is the point of open source.

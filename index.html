The Methodology

Five Variables.
One Score.

The CBI is not politically neutral. It is built on the documented reality that communities of color, low-income communities, Disabled communities, and communities with limited English proficiency bear disproportionate climate burdens they did not create.

Every weight decision and variable selection reflects that baseline. The Social Vulnerability Index is weighted highest because adaptive capacity — the ability to prepare, respond, and recover — determines outcomes more than physical exposure alone.

Why This Exists
"A neighborhood's climate risk is never just about weather. It's about who has the resources to prepare for and recover from it."

I was born Disabled. My wife acquired disabilities and is now chronically ill. We live in Philadelphia and depend on SEPTA — a transit system that is underfunded, aging, and increasingly climate-stressed.

When I designed the Social Vulnerability weight at 25% — the highest in the index — I was thinking about what it means to not be able to evacuate during a flood because the accessible transit routes are flooded. The CBI treats Disabled communities as a primary equity concern, not a footnote.

Every methodology decision is documented and open to critique. If you disagree with a decision, open an issue. That is the point of open source.
